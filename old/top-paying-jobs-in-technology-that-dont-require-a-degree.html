Top paying jobs in technology that don't require a degree

While a degree in technology can be beneficial for certain career paths, it is not always necessary to have a formal education to pursue a high-paying job in the field. Here are some of the top paying jobs in technology that do not require a degree:

Software developer: Software developers design, develop, and maintain software applications and systems. They are in high demand and can earn a median salary of $105,590 per year, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Technical support specialist: Technical support specialists provide technical assistance and support to customers or users of a company's products or services. They typically have a strong understanding of computers and technology and can earn a median salary of $53,470 per year.

Network administrator: Network administrators are responsible for maintaining and troubleshooting a company's computer networks. They typically have a strong understanding of computer hardware and software and can earn a median salary of $82,050 per year.

Information security analyst: Information security analysts are responsible for protecting an organization's computer systems and networks from cyber threats. They typically have a strong understanding of computer security and can earn a median salary of $99,730 per year.

Database administrator: Database administrators are responsible for designing, implementing, and maintaining an organization's database systems. They typically have a strong understanding of database management and can earn a median salary of $93,750 per year.

While a degree can be beneficial for certain career paths in technology, these jobs demonstrate that it is not always necessary to have a formal education to pursue a high-paying career in the field. Experience, skills, and certifications can often be more important than a degree for certain positions.
